# Phase Overview

## Purpose

Provide a structured view of the investigation lifecycle and how each phase contributes to evidence integrity and analysis.

## Phase Structure

Phase 00 — Initial Triage
Determine if investigation is required

Phase 01 — Baseline
Capture system state for comparison

Phase 02 — Suspicion
Identify persistence, network anomalies, and early indicators

Phase 03 — Preservation
Secure and document evidence before modification

Phase 05 — Analysis
Perform deep inspection of persistence, artifacts, and binaries

Phase 06 — Correlation
Link processes, persistence mechanisms, and network behavior

Phase 08 — Validation
Confirm findings and detect regrowth or recurring activity

Phase 09 — Reporting
Document findings and prepare defensible output

## Key Principle

Phases are ordered to protect evidence and reduce the risk of contamination or loss.

## Notes

Skipping or reordering phases may result in incomplete or non-defensible findings.
